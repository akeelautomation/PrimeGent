Affiliate disclosure

How PrimeGent makes money from product links

PrimeGent may earn commissions from qualifying purchases made through some outbound product links. This page explains what that means and what it does not mean.

Affiliate relationship notice

Some links on PrimeGent are affiliate links. If you click one of those links and make a qualifying purchase, PrimeGent may earn a commission from the retailer at no additional cost to you.

Why this disclosure exists

Affiliate compensation can create a commercial relationship between a publisher and a retailer. Readers should know when that relationship exists so they can evaluate recommendations with the right context.

How products are chosen

PrimeGent aims to recommend products based on usefulness, versatility, fit, category relevance, and how well a piece supports the wardrobe advice published in the journal. A commission opportunity should not be the only reason a product appears on the site.

No extra cost to readers

Using an affiliate link does not increase the listed purchase price for the visitor. The commission is paid by the retailer when the retailer's qualifying terms are met.

Retailers and third parties

Once you click through to Amazon or another merchant, that platform controls the shopping experience, pricing, availability, and any data collection tied to the transaction. Review the merchant's own policies before buying.

Questions about disclosures

If you need clarification about affiliate links or how PrimeGent handles monetized recommendations, use the contact page and include the page URL you are asking about.
